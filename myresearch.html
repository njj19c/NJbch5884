Biosensors and Scanning Electrochemical Probe Microscopies

Nathaniel Jones (Lazenby Group)

In Dr. Lazenby's laboratory, we focus on research involving micro and nanosensor technologies, as well as scanning electrochemical probe microscopies (SEPMs). While I have done recent work using macro-scale Pt working electrodes to characterize the electroactivity of members of the Lanthanide series, via cyclic voltammetry, my primary research project involves the fabrication of an ATP sensor within a nanopipette.These nanopipettes are pulled using a P-2000 CO2 Laser Puller at a variety of parameteters, depending on whether they are borosilicate or quartz glass.

Once fabricated, these sensors should not only be capable of reversible ATP detection in solution, but also be suitable to use in scanning ion conductance microscopy (SICM), whereby I intend to map out the ATP activity above various cell types.
